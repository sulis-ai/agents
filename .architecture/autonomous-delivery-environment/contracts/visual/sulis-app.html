Sulis app — visual contract

The Sulis cockpit, redrawn on your own experience fragments — the same calm rhythm as the-morning-calm, with the RAG status pattern from the-org-heatmap. Warm paper, stone and near-black do the work, in Inter with room to breathe. The brand is worn lightly: the real sunset mark sits top-left as the logo, and when an accent is needed the surface leans on the bluer palette — blue for the main action, a single teal touch for "this is the live thing." The gold is kept for the logo only, not splashed across the screen. Your own messages sit in a quiet neutral bubble, not a coloured one. The same surface is shown in light and dark. The parts make one experience: the board you land on — each stage column now carrying its colour as a small dot in the header; a change opened as a thread that shows the agent's work (the files it edits, the tests it runs) interleaved with the chat, with checkpoints you can roll back to and a clear paused state when it needs you; a right-hand panel that flips between Preview, Files and Brain, one at a time; the go-ahead step before anything consequential; a full Files explorer (browse the whole change's folders, or flip to just what changed — each file marked new, edited or removed — and see the exact diff), search, and the three states every screen must have (loading, empty, something-went-wrong). State markers are now simply a coloured dot plus a word — no busy shapes. The layout structure is grounded in well-shipped tools (GitHub Projects, Replit Agent, Lovable, VS Code); every colour and font stays yours.

The sunset mark stays — this is where the brand lives. Everything else leans blue/teal.
