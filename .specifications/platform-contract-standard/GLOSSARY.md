# Glossary — Platform Contract Standard

The locked vocabulary for this specification. Every artifact uses these terms with
these meanings.

## Preferred terms

### Platform Contract
A design-time, version-controlled artifact that captures what a **third-party
platform we do not control** (GitHub Actions, AWS, Stripe, …) guarantees and
requires, grounded so that every claim about the platform's behaviour is traceable
to an official source. It is the fourth design-stage contract, sibling to the Data
contract, the Visual contract, and the ServiceSpec.
**Also known as:** the platform seam contract.
**NOT the same as:** Data contract / Visual contract / ServiceSpec (see below).

### Bound claim
A single statement the contract makes about the platform, **bound** to the specific
official-documentation source that justifies it. In harness terms, an entry in the
claim→source binding table. The unit of a Platform Contract is the bound claim, not
the paragraph.

### Source citation
The inline reference attached to a bound claim: an **official-doc URL + retrieval
date + verbatim quote** from the source. The quote (not just the URL) is what makes
the claim checkable against meaning, not merely existence.

### Flagged inference
A span of contract content that is **not** the expansion of a bound claim —
connective text, reasoning, or a recommendation derived from the bound claims. It
is explicitly marked `inferred: true` (or "unattributed") and **never** carries a
source citation. Legitimate and necessary; it must just be labelled as ours, not
the platform's.
**Also known as:** unattributed span.
**NOT the same as:** a bound claim. Conflating the two is MUC-006.

### Probe (a.k.a. ablation)
A real, minimal **sandbox exercise** that confirms both "the doc says X" **and**
"the platform actually does X" — for a claim the integration design depends on. The
empirical leg of a load-bearing claim, complementing its citation. Produces a
`probe-result` (confirmed / refuted) with evidence the exercise ran.
**Also known as:** ablation probe (borrowed from the harness's leave-one-out
attribution lineage).

### manifest-insufficient
The state the faithful-generation-harness escalates to when a **load-bearing claim
cannot be grounded** in the official-documentation manifest. Borrowed from the
harness `binding-table-incomplete-or-invalid` failure mode. The harness **refuses**
to assert the behaviour as fact and records it as a flagged assumption requiring a
probe. This is the gate that would have caught the reusable-workflow incident.

### Grounded vs. assumed
A claim is **grounded** when it is bound to an official source whose *meaning*
supports it (and, if load-bearing, probed). A claim is **assumed** when it has no
official source — it is recorded as a flagged inference / assumption requiring a
probe, never asserted as documented fact. The whole standard is the discipline of
keeping these two visibly distinct.

### The gate
The design-stage block: a change touching a gated third-party platform cannot
proceed past design without a Platform Contract. Enforced by skill prose
(`/sulis:specify`, `/sulis:draft-architecture`) **and** mechanically by the rubric
phase P-PLAT, so a prose edit alone cannot bypass it (MUC-004).

### P-PLAT
The new decompose-validation-rubric phase (sibling to Phase 7 ServiceSpec and Phase
9 P-VER) that fails a cross-kind / integration Work Package set touching a gated
third party without a referenced Platform Contract.

### faithful-generation-harness
The mandated brain Workflow that produces a Platform Contract. A five-step
OODA-grounding discipline: observe-manifest → orient-select-relevant →
decide-commit-bindings → act-generate-from-bindings → self-critique-grounding. Its
load-bearing property: content is generated by **expanding only committed
bindings**, so every span is causally traceable to its source — faithful by
construction.

## The four contracts — disambiguation table

| Contract | Standard | Seam (between) | We control it? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Data contract** | `CONTRACT_FIRST_STANDARD.md` | backend ↔ frontend (producer ↔ consumer, machines) | Yes |
| **Visual contract** | `UX_VISUAL_DESIGN_STANDARD.md` | product ↔ user (human) | Yes |
| **ServiceSpec** | `architecture/SERVICE_SPECIFICATION.md` | service ↔ agent / SDK | Yes |
| **Platform Contract** | `PLATFORM_CONTRACT_STANDARD.md` (this) | our system ↔ third-party platform | **No** |

The distinguishing axis: the first three describe seams **we author**, so the
"contract" is our design decision. The Platform Contract describes a seam **the
third party authors** — so the contract is a *faithful capture* of their reality,
which is why it must be source-bound and refuse fabrication.

## NOT the same as

- **Bound claim** vs. **flagged inference** — the former carries a source; the
  latter must not. (MUC-006 is the conflation.)
- **Grounded** vs. **assumed** — grounded has an official source whose meaning
  supports it; assumed has none and is flagged. (MUC-001/002 attack this line.)
- **Source exists** vs. **source supports the claim** — a real URL is necessary but
  not sufficient; the verbatim quote + meaning-check distinguish them. (MUC-002.)
- **Probe run** vs. **probe recorded** — recording `confirmed` is not the same as
  executing the exercise; evidence distinguishes them. (MUC-005.)
